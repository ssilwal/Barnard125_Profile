Before he even arrived at Columbia, Nathan Chan, CC/Juilliard ’14 and a founder of campus cello ensemble String Theory, had a résumé that would wow most music professionals. The San Francisco Bay Area native made his conducting debut at age three with the San José Chamber Orchestra, performed as a soloist with a host of international orchestras, and even starred in “The Music In Me,” a Peabody Award-winning documentary that aired on HBO in 2006. So when it came to higher education, he wanted an institution that challenged him in the classroom as well as in the auditorium.
